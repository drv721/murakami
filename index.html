Act I · The Origin · 01 of 02
Where it
started.
Albert Einstein Hospital. The Bronx. Mom in surgery. Sox game on mute. Phone in lap. Nowhere to be.

The White Sox announcer called Murakami the Southside Samurai — the team took issue. A friend in Pennsylvania thought we could do better. “We should trademark Southside Shogun.”

He was joking. Probably.

One prompt to ChatGPT. Fifteen minutes. Four concept art pieces. A friend texted back from Pennsylvania: “BRO. This is actually REAL.”

That text is when a joke became a thought experiment.

Act I · The Origin · 02 of 02
Where it
went.
More products. More concepts. Both colorways. Hat variations, hoodie variations, sleeveless jerseys. ChatGPT kept up — until it didn’t.

Text on blank slides. No design. No story. That’s when Claude entered — not for the images, but for the thinking.

Late night. The Bronx. A conversation about what a silk bomber jacket could be — 80s MLB satin meets Japanese sukajan. That wasn’t in the brief. It emerged from the conversation.

That’s how the best ideas always work.
